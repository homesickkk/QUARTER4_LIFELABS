C. How was the community affected by the typhoon?

Super Typhoon Rai, also known as Bagyong Odette started its first landfall on December 16, 2021, rain was heavily pouring, strong winds, and floods in the Visayas and Mindanao islands. Super Typhoon Rai made 9 landfalls in seven different provinces and communities in the provinces of Agusan del Norte, Dinagat Islands, Surigao del Norte, Surigao del Sur, Leyte and Southern Leyte had to face the impact of the Typhoon. It caused significant damage to health facilities, schools, and essential services and also heightened the risk of communicable disease outbreaks. The typhoon demolished lives and devastated farming and fishing communities which were providing a major source of income and livelihoods. It caused massive electricity and telecommunications outages that continued to affect many lives. Rai exited the Philippines on 18 December as the strongest storm to hit Mindanao in 10 years and the 3rd ever strongest recorded storm in the Northern Hemisphere leaving an estimate of 13 million people affected by Typhoon Rai.

D. How did the community prepare for it? How did they recover?

Typhoon Rai (Bagyong Odette) was one of the strongest typhoons to hit the Philippines in 2021, and communities prepared through early warnings, pre-emptive evacuations, and government assistance. The Philippines Atmospheric Geophysical and Astronomical Services Administration (PAGASA) issued advisories, while local governments set up evacuation centers and stockpiled relief goods. Despite these efforts, Odette caused widespread devastation, displacing thousands and damaging homes, roads, and power lines. Recovery efforts included relief operations led by the government, NGOs, and private sectors, providing food, clean water, and shelter. International aid also played a big role, with countries and organization donating financial assistance and resources for rebuilding. Communities demonstrated resilience by organizing mutual aid efforts, fundraising, and rebuilding their homes together.

G. Was the typhoon accompanied by secondary disasters? How did they affect the community?

Typhoon Rai (Bagyong Odette) not only brought devastating winds and rainfall but also triggered secondary disasters such as flooding and landslides. These events exacerbated the damage to infrastructure, homes, and livelihoods. The destruction of agricultural lands and fishing equipment severely impacted local economies, leaving many communities without their primary sources of income. Recovery efforts have been challenging, with millions requiring humanities assistance, including shelter, clean water, and healthcare . The compounded effects of these secondary disasters underscore the vulnerability of affected regions and the critical need for comprehensive disaster preparedness and response strategies.

How does the typhoon develop over time?

On December 16, 2021, a huge typhoon suddenly struck at different places inside and outside the Philippines. Thousands of buildings, houses, trees, and lives were destroyed. This typhoon was the 15th storm to hit the Philippines and was the strongest one in the year 2021. This typhoon is called the typhoon rai or what they call bagyong odette in local areas. This super typhoon originated from a disturbance in the ocean near the equator during December 10 (6 days before it became a super typhoon), creating a low-pressure area near the part where another one was scattered on the previous day. After this, things got worse for it slowly became a tropical depression on December 12. Then, it continuously created a typhoon, and was slowly turned into a super typhoon.
